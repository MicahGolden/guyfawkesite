father, son, and the holy dollar

everyone is always trying to sell you something whether it be this asshole and his poems or the man at the bar who knows you've had too much but wants a tip and you'll write 40% on the receipt all the same everyone is always trying to sell you something its the shameless salesman in the mall suggesting financing its the woman who tells you you're the only one she'll ever love and you'll give her love all the same our transactional society commodifies life, it commodifies death, people sell their bodies with pride people sell their thoughts without shame the homeless work for food like it's a luxury instead of a foundation of being alive the common man sells his time to a corporation that lacks empathy and gives less than a shit about him everyone is always trying to sell something to live to take advantage of those who will buy every building exists to generate profit no good exists when money is involved no heart exists when the almighty dollar is the backing poets write what others want to hear singers sing songs pre-penned by better singers and retail employees peddle products and credit cards they don't believe in but we'll buy it all the same
