the cannibalization of ego

i bought a rose and a bottle of wine that afternoon i had planned on asking her to picnic with me at the lake something i'm not known for but i'd found the courage the scene was set in my mind steak, i'll read a stupid poem i wrote about how she reminds me of some aspect of nature (probably a flower i haven't compared another woman to) she'll swoon, because who else would write a heartfelt poem for her? certainly not adam (god i fucking hate that guy) and at the end of it, i'll uncork the wine and pour us both a glass, and if i'm feeling particularly ballsy we'll share a cigarette we'll gaze into the water our eyes will meet in the rippling reflection she'll turn to me and i to her she'll say i'm the only one who's ever made her feel this way she tells me i'm enough in fact i'm all she's ever wanted i'm perfect she'd say and the sun will set as soon as our lips meet i'll place the rose gently in her hair and we will be one my tattered heart suddenly sewn together again there it was, the perfect first date this was going to be the one that would finally turn my life around one great romantic tale about a downtrodden guy becoming brand new because of a woman. as i was having these thoughts, i realized i had devolved her into an idealistic pastiche of who she really was it was 7 o clock, and i got into my car, drove to the lake and parked. i unfurled the picnic blanket just as the sun was beginning to set lying down and watching the water flow and flow i decided not to invite her that night this wasn't the right time for love it wouldn't fix me and it couldn't, even as badly as i wanted it to the realization only struck me after it was too late to turn back but before i had made the mistake of asking her to come now i was alone at the lake a rose in my hair an empty bottle of wine and the cannibalization of ego
